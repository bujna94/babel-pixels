About

Every 3D object is just a grid of voxels. Each cell is either on or off, giving 2cells total arrangements.

The total number of possible shapes is incomprehensibly large. Somewhere in that space is, for example, a hammer. The most efficient boat turbine ever designed. A model of your future home. A 3D scan of a face you haven't met yet.

Hit Random Combination — you'll get noise. But in theory, keep hitting it long enough and you'd eventually see any object imaginable. Don't hold your breath.

Drag to rotate · Scroll to zoom · Right-drag to pan

Every image is just a grid of pixels. A screen-sized image at 1920×1080 in RGB has ~6.2 million pixels, each with 16.7 million possible colours.

The total number of possible images is 2566,220,800 — a number with millions of digits. Somewhere in that space is every photo ever taken, every painting, every frame of every film. And your face, right now, reading this.

Hit Random Image — you'll get noise. But in theory, keep hitting it long enough and you'd eventually see the Mona Lisa. Don't hold your breath.
